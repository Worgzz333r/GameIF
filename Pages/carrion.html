Description

Developer: Phobia Game Studio
Publisher: Devolver Digital
Composer: Chris Velasco
Genre: Horror-game, platformer
Release date: July 23. 2020

Carrion is a computer indie game developed by the Polish Phobia Game Studio and released by Devolver Digital. The developers described it as a “horror in reverse”, reviewers described it as a platformer with elements of metroidvania.

In Carrion, the player controls an amorphous monster with many tentacles, similar to the creature from the movie The Thing, and must navigate an underground laboratory, punching his way through and killing people along the way.

Gameplay

Players control a red amorphous monster with tentacles. The monster must make its way through a facility with many obstacles and closed doors, killing and devouring scientists and soldiers.

Over the course of the game, the creature can find various enhancements in the game world - such as the ability to smash wooden barricades. The size of the creature changes depending on its health - after eating enough victims, the monster grows in size and acquires a new form, and with it, new abilities.

Development and production

Carrion, developed by the Polish indie studio Phobia Game Studio, was first revealed at GDC 2018. The game's haunting soundtrack by Chris Velasco was released alongside the game in 2020.

Phobia Game studio released a demo of the game on Steam in October 2019. The game was released for PC (Windows, Linux and macOS), Nintendo Switch and Xbox One consoles on July 23, 2020. It was previously reported that the game was planned to be released for PlayStation 4 as well, but no release date was announced for PlayStation 4.
