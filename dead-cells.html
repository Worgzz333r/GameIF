Description

Developer: Motion Twin, Evil Empire
Publisher: Motion Twin, Playdigious
Director: Brad Fay Jr. Barbara Johanhoff
Genre: Roguelike, Metroidvania
Release date: Aug 17. 2018

Dead Cells is a 2018 roguelike-Metroidvania game developed by Motion Twin and Evil Empire, and published by Motion Twin.

The player takes the role of an amorphous creature called the Prisoner. As the Prisoner, the player must fight their way out of a diseased island in order to slay the island's King. The player gains weapons, treasure and other tools through exploration of the procedurally-generated levels.

Gameplay

Dead Cells is a 2D roguevania where you play as a prisoner exploring a monster-filled island. You lose items when you die, but some remain. Weapons include swords, bows, shields, and traps. In combat, you can dodge to avoid attacks, jump over them, or slam into the ground.

The game's combat is similar to Dark Souls, with difficult enemies and frequent deaths. As you explore levels and fight creatures, you can collect Cells to purchase permanent upgrades, such as potions and weapons. These Cells can only be spent at the end of a dungeon section. You can also find blueprints to unlock new upgrades. Levels are procedurally generated, and you can obtain mutations and reforge weapons between dungeons. You can also find Power Scrolls to increase your hit points and weapon damage, and Runes to unlock new methods of travel.

Plot and story

The player controls the Prisoner, an immortal creature that can possess dead bodies on an unnamed island. Bodies can die, forcing the Prisoner back to find another. The Prisoner communicates through gestures and body language, but you can see its thoughts.

The Prisoner awakens in the depths of the island's prison, suffering from amnesia. A soldier encounters the Prisoner, and mentions that they can no longer die. The Prisoner tries to escape the prison, but their head is forced back to the depths as soon as its body is destroyed. Between subsequent escape attempts, the Prisoner learns that the island was once a mighty kingdom that fell when a plague known as "The Malaise" transformed most of the kingdom's citizens into mutated monsters.
